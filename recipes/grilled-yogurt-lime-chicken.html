Grilled Yogurt Lime Chicken

Luna Thyme | January 19, 2026

Tender chicken cutlets are bathed in a zesty cilantro-lime yogurt marinade that infuses bright citrus, fresh herbs, and mild spice into every bite. Marinating for several hours makes the chicken juicy and flavorful, and quick grilling ensures a delicious, healthy weeknight supper that pairs effortlessly with salads, rice, or grilled veggies.

You’ll Need

  • ½ cup loosely packed fresh cilantro leaves (with tender stems)
  • ½ cup plain yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1½ teaspoons ground coriander
  • Juice of ½ small lime (plus lime wedges for serving, optional)
  • 1 clove garlic, coarsely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • ½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1½ pounds boneless, skinless chicken breasts, patted dry and sliced into cutlets
  • Olive oil spray (optional for grilling)

Let’s Get Started!

  1. Make the marinade
    • In a blender or food processor, combine:
      • Cilantro leaves and tender stems
      • Plain yogurt
      • Olive oil
      • Ground coriander
      • Lime juice
      • Garlic
      • Kosher salt
      • Black pepper
    • Blend until smooth and creamy.
  2. Marinate the chicken
    • Place the chicken cutlets in a large bowl or resealable bag.
    • Pour the marinade over the chicken and toss to coat evenly.
    • Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes (up to 8 hours for deeper flavor).
  3. Preheat the grill
    • Preheat a grill or grill pan to medium-high heat.
    • Lightly oil the grates or spray with olive oil spray to prevent sticking.
  4. Grill the chicken
    • Remove the chicken from the marinade, allowing excess to drip off.
    • Place on the hot grill and cook for 4–5 minutes per side, until grill marks form and the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C).
  5. Rest the chicken
    • Transfer the chicken to a plate and let it rest for 5 minutes to keep it juicy.
  6. Serve
    • Serve warm with lime wedges on the side for extra brightness.
    • Pairs well with grilled vegetables, rice, or a fresh salad.
