Spicy Coconut Curry Chicken

Cyrus Brine

This Coconut Curry Chicken is a creamy, one-pan curry where juicy pieces of chicken simmer in a spiced tomato-coconut sauce infused with warm curry powder, garlic and ginger. Ready in about 30 minutes, it’s rich and comforting with a gentle sweetness from coconut milk balanced by acidity from tomato and lime — perfect served over rice for an easy weeknight meal.

What This Recipe Uses

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 teaspoons ginger paste (or grated fresh ginger)
  • 2 teaspoons minced garlic
  • 2 pounds skinless, boneless chicken thighs (or chicken breast), cut into bite-size pieces
  • ½ teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons curry powder (mild or hot)
  • 1 cup tomato puree (or puree of fresh Roma tomatoes)
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • ½ cup water
  • 14 ounces (1 can) coconut milk
  • 1 teaspoon coconut palm sugar (optional; honey can be substituted)
  • 1 teaspoon lime juice
  • 2 tablespoons chopped cilantro or parsley

Let’s Begin Cooking!

  1. Prepare the aromatics
    • Heat 1 tablespoon olive oil in a large skillet or saucepan over medium heat.
    • Add the finely chopped onion and sauté for 3–4 minutes, stirring often, until soft and translucent.
  2. Build the flavor base
    • Add the ginger paste and minced garlic to the onions.
    • Cook for 30–60 seconds, stirring constantly, until fragrant (be careful not to burn them).
  3. Add the chicken
    • Add the bite-size chicken pieces to the pan.
    • Sprinkle with ½ teaspoon salt and stir well to coat the chicken in the aromatics.
    • Cook for 4–5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chicken is lightly browned on the outside (it does not need to be fully cooked yet).
  4. Toast the curry spices
    • Sprinkle in the curry powder and stir well.
    • Cook for 1 minute to bloom the spices and deepen their flavor.
  5. Add tomatoes and liquid
    • Stir in the tomato puree, tomato paste, and ½ cup water.
    • Mix thoroughly until smooth and well combined.
    • Let the mixture simmer for 5 minutes, allowing the sauce to thicken slightly.
  6. Add coconut milk and sweetness
    • Pour in the coconut milk and stir gently to combine.
    • Add the coconut palm sugar (or honey, if using).
    • Reduce heat to low, cover partially, and simmer for 15–20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chicken is fully cooked and the sauce is rich and creamy.
  7. Finish with brightness
    • Stir in the lime juice and taste the curry.
    • Adjust salt or sweetness if needed.
  8. Garnish and serve
    • Remove from heat and sprinkle with chopped cilantro or parsley.
    • Serve hot with steamed rice, naan, or flatbread.
