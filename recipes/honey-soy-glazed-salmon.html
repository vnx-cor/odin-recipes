Honey Soy Glazed Salmon

Marin Saffron | February 11, 2026

This Honey Garlic Soy Glazed Salmon is a simple yet flavor-packed dinner that combines sweet honey and aromatic garlic with salty soy and fresh ginger to create a sticky, savory glaze that caramelizes beautifully as the salmon roasts. Ready in about 35 minutes with minimal prep and just 6 core ingredients, it’s both easy enough for a weeknight and polished enough for guests.

Gather These

  • 4 salmon fillets (about 6 ounces / 170 g each)
  • 1/3 cup (80 ml) reduced-sodium soy sauce (or regular)
  • 1/3 cup (113 g) honey
  • 1 tablespoon (15 ml) sesame oil (or olive oil)
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced (or ~2 teaspoons jarred/minced)
  • 1 teaspoon peeled, minced fresh ginger
  • Chopped green onion and/or sesame seeds

Let’s Get in the Kitchen!

  1. Prepare the glaze
    • In a small bowl, whisk together:
      • Soy sauce
      • Honey
      • Sesame oil
      • Minced garlic
      • Minced ginger
    • Set aside.
  2. Prepare the salmon
    • Pat the salmon fillets dry with paper towels.
    • This helps the fish sear and prevents sticking.
  3. Heat the pan
    • Heat a large nonstick or stainless-steel skillet over medium heat.
    • Lightly coat the pan with a small amount of oil if needed.
  4. Sear the salmon
    • Place the salmon fillets skin-side down (if they have skin) in the hot pan.
    • Cook for 4–5 minutes without moving them, until the skin is crispy and the salmon releases easily from the pan.
  5. Add the glaze
    • Flip the salmon carefully.
    • Pour the prepared honey soy glaze into the pan around the fillets.
    • Reduce heat to medium-low.
  6. Glaze and cook
    • Spoon the sauce over the salmon continuously for 2–4 minutes, until the salmon is cooked through and the sauce thickens into a glossy glaze.
  7. Finish and garnish
    • Remove from heat.
    • Sprinkle with chopped green onions and/or sesame seeds.
  8. Serve
    • Serve immediately over steamed rice, quinoa, or alongside vegetables.
