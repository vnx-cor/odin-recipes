Garlic Butter Shrimp Pasta

Tessa Ember

Garlic Butter Shrimp Pasta is a quick yet elegant Italian-inspired dish where al dente long pasta meets succulent shrimp sautéed in a rich garlic butter sauce accented with bright lemon and savory Parmesan. Simple pantry ingredients are transformed into a restaurant-style meal in about 15–25 minutes, making this a perfect weeknight favorite that’s luxurious without being complicated.

What Goes In

  • Fettuccine or long pasta such as spaghetti or linguine
  • Shrimp (fresh or frozen, peeled and deveined; tails on for presentation)
  • Garlic cloves (fresh minced recommended)
  • Unsalted butter (provides richness)
  • Lemon juice and lemon zest (bright acidity)
  • Olive or other cooking oil (to prevent butter burning and sear shrimp)
  • Parmesan cheese (creamy, umami finish)
  • Salt
  • Black pepper (season to taste)
  • Fresh parsley (chopped)

Ready to Cook?

  1. Cook the pasta
    • Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
    • Cook the fettuccine (or other long pasta) according to package directions until al dente.
    • Reserve ½ cup of pasta water, then drain and set the pasta aside.
  2. Prepare the shrimp
    • Pat the shrimp dry with paper towels.
    • Season lightly with salt and black pepper.
  3. Sear the shrimp
    • Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat.
    • Add a drizzle of olive oil and 1 tablespoon butter.
    • Add the shrimp in a single layer and cook for 1–2 minutes per side, until pink and opaque.
    • Remove shrimp from the pan and set aside.
  4. Make the garlic butter sauce
    • Reduce heat to medium.
    • Add another 1–2 tablespoons butter to the same skillet.
    • Add the minced garlic and sauté for 30 seconds, just until fragrant (do not brown).
  5. Add lemon
    • Stir in lemon juice and lemon zest, scraping up any browned bits from the pan.
  6. Combine pasta and sauce
    • Add the cooked pasta to the skillet and toss to coat.
    • Add a splash of the reserved pasta water to loosen the sauce if needed.
  7. Return shrimp to the pan
    • Add the shrimp back to the skillet and gently toss to warm through, about 1 minute.
  8. Finish with cheese and herbs
    • Remove from heat and sprinkle in grated Parmesan cheese and chopped parsley.
    • Season with additional salt and black pepper to taste.
  9. Serve
    • Serve immediately with extra Parmesan and a touch of lemon zest if desired.
