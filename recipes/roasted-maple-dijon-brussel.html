Roasted Maple Dijon Brussel

Oren Basil | August 6, 2026

These Roasted Maple Dijon Brussels Sprouts are a simple yet flavorful side dish that pairs the earthy, caramelized goodness of roasted sprouts with a tangy-sweet glaze of Dijon mustard and pure maple syrup. The roasting process creates beautifully browned edges, and the bright balance of sweet and savory makes this recipe a standout alongside meats, grains, or holiday spreads.

What to Prep

  • Brussels sprouts — fresh, trimmed and halved lengthwise
  • Pure maple syrup — for sweetness and glaze
  • Dijon mustard — tangy flavor base
  • Olive oil — for roasting and caramelization
  • Salt — to season
  • Black pepper — to taste

Let’s Whip This Up!

  1. Preheat the oven
    • Preheat your oven to 400°F (205°C).
    • Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or lightly grease it with olive oil.
  2. Prepare the Brussels sprouts
    • Wash the Brussels sprouts, trim the stems, and cut them in half lengthwise.
    • Pat them dry to help with proper roasting and caramelization.
  3. Make the maple Dijon glaze
    • In a small bowl, whisk together:
      • Maple syrup
      • Dijon mustard
      • Olive oil
    • Mix until smooth and well combined.
  4. Season the Brussels sprouts
    • Place the halved Brussels sprouts on the prepared baking sheet.
    • Drizzle the maple Dijon glaze over them.
    • Season with salt and black pepper.
    • Toss well until all sprouts are evenly coated.
  5. Roast
    • Spread the Brussels sprouts in a single layer, cut-side down for best browning.
    • Roast for 25–30 minutes, flipping once halfway through, until tender inside and caramelized on the outside.
  6. Finish and serve
    • Remove from the oven and taste.
    • Adjust seasoning with additional salt or pepper if needed.
    • Serve warm as a side dish.
